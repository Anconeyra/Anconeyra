🔐 Security Policy

Last Updated:

Responsible Disclosure Policy

If you discover a security vulnerability in this website or any related services, I appreciate responsible disclosure.

📋 Guidelines

  • Do: Report vulnerabilities privately via email or PGP-encrypted message
  • Do: Provide detailed reproduction steps
  • Do: Allow reasonable time for remediation before public disclosure
  • Don't: Exploit vulnerabilities beyond what's necessary to demonstrate them
  • Don't: Access or modify other users' data
  • Don't: Use excessive force when testing (DoS, brute force, etc.)

📧 Contact Methods

  • Email: security@anconeyra.dev
  • PGP: Download Public Key
  • Matrix: @tuusuario:matrix.org

🏆 Recognition

I believe in acknowledging security researchers who help improve security. Unless you prefer to remain anonymous, I will:

  • Acknowledge your contribution in security advisories
  • Link to your website/social media (if desired)
  • Provide a public hall of fame for significant findings

⚠️ Legal Safe Harbor

Research conducted in accordance with this policy will be considered authorized and I will not pursue legal action. If legal action is initiated by a third party, I will make it known that your research was authorized.

📝 Scope

In Scope:

  • This website (anconeyra.dev / anconeyra.github.io)
  • Subdomains and related services
  • Mobile applications (if applicable)

Out of Scope:

  • Third-party services linked from this site
  • Social engineering attacks against staff
  • Physical security attacks
  • DDoS attacks

🔒 Known Security Measures

This site implements the following security controls:

  • Content Security Policy (CSP)
  • X-Frame-Options: DENY
  • X-Content-Type-Options: nosniff
  • HTTPS enforcement
  • No third-party trackers
  • Regular security audits
